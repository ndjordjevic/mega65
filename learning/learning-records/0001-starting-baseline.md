# Starting baseline: digital-logic foundation, new to HDL/6502

The learner disclosed a working background in **digital logic / electronics** (comfortable with gates,
registers, datasheets) but is **new to HDL (VHDL/Verilog) and the 6502 family**. Stated learning
preference: **conceptual-first, then deep** — build architecture intuition before touching VHDL.

Why it matters for future sessions:
- Don't re-teach gates/registers/boolean logic — pitch above absolute beginner on digital fundamentals.
- Introduce HDL and 6502 concepts from scratch when reached.
- The end-goal (read every line of `mega65-core` VHDL; write own gateware) means the FPGA/VHDL arc is the
  destination, not an aside — but it is deliberately deferred until the hardware mental model is solid.

Evidence: direct answers during mission interview (see [[MISSION.md]]). Not yet demonstrated through exercises.
