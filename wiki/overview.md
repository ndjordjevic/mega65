# MEGA65 retro computer — overview

The official site [[mega65.org]] establishes the foundation of this domain: the MEGA65 is an open-source FPGA realization of the unreleased Commodore 65 — a real 8-bit computer roughly 40x faster than a C64, highly compatible with both C64 and C65, shipping with the MEGA-OS hypervisor, BASIC 65, 4 SIDs, dual SD slots, and Ethernet. The site maps the entire official ecosystem: four free PDF manuals (User's Guide, BASIC 65 Reference, Developer's Guide, and the 1,433-page Complete Compendium), the Confluence documentation wiki, the Filehost software repository, the Xemu emulator onboarding path (including ROM patching and the 263-title ALL_INTROS package), the alternative-cores catalog that reflashes the FPGA into other machines, and the community channels (Discord, Forum64, Reddit, YouTube).

[[dansanderson.com]] is the ecosystem's premier onboarding and tutorial hub, run by Steering Committee member Dan Sanderson. Its Welcome Guide supplies the new-owner mental model — SD card conventions, FPGA cores in 7 slots, the `MEGA65.ROM` file, the Hypervisor, PAL/NTSC — while the Cross Development series and monthly Digest cover the developer workflow: write on a modern host, cross-assemble with Acme (native 45GS02 support) or Kick Assembler (via fork), convert BASIC with `petcat -w65` or compile XC=BASIC, then run in Xemu (`-prg`) or push to hardware via JTAG/M65Connect. It also documents the PRG file format and the trick of prepending a BASIC `SYS` launcher to machine-code programs.

[[retrocombs.com]] adds the getting-started / how-to layer: Steven Combs' 32 hands-on blog posts cover setup, configuration, running Xemu on every OS (Mac/ChromeOS/uConsole), building a MEGA65 on a Nexys FPGA board, patching the original C65 ROM, BASIC 65 conversions, and accessories — anchored by his chapter-by-chapter video-and-blog companion to the official User's Guide. He also maintains a curated resource page of download links (latest `MEGA65.ROM`, SD Card Essentials, GEOS 65 beta, the full-featured C64 core) and tools (Xemu, M65 Connect, `m65`/`mega65_ftp`). The consumer-friendly, operational complement to the more programming-focused [[dansanderson.com]].

[[retrocogs.mega65.com]] supplies the register-level graphics knowledge: VIC-IV screen layout via CHRCOUNT/DISPROWS/LINESTEP, H640/H320 and V400/V200 scaling semantics ($D031), hardware scrolling, FCM/NCM color modes, and the Raster Rewrite Buffer — a 2048-pixel line buffer with GOTOX characters that enables layered backgrounds and software sprites ("pixies") under Super Extended Attribute Mode. Each tutorial maps to working Kick Assembler code in the companion Mega65Tutorials repo.

[[wiebow.mega65.com]] contributes the most concrete build pipeline: Kick Assembler's 65CE02/45GS02 fork (`.cpu _45gs02`), `c1541` for D81 images, Xemu for fast iteration, and Ethernet deployment to real hardware via `mega65_ftp -e` and `etherload -5` — automated in one `make.sh` for a ~3-second edit-build-run loop, with the Matrix Mode Monitor (MEGA+TAB) for debugging.

[[files.mega65.org]] is the official software distribution point — games, demos, utilities, ROMs, cores, manuals, and owner articles — browsed live rather than statically captured, with account-gated downloads and owner-only ROMs.

[[mega65.atlassian.net]] is the collaborative Confluence wiki: BASIC 65 how-tos, compatible hardware, system development notes, and the canonical release notes (0.97 "10th Anniversary Edition" and the 0.97.2 R6A bugfix release as of early 2026); its Ethernet-tools and petcat pages back the workflows described by [[wiebow.mega65.com]] and [[dansanderson.com]].

[[MEGA65-mega65-user-guide]] is the wiki's authority layer: the LaTeX source of all five official books in one repo, kept here as a full local clone so the entire spec is greppable — 45GS02 instruction set, VIC-IV/SID/Ethernet/CIA register appendices, memory map, hypervisor calls, KERNAL jump table, and the complete BASIC 65 keyword reference. For any official-spec question, grep these `.tex` files before searching the web.

[[RetroCogs-Mega65Tutorials]] turns [[retrocogs.mega65.com]]'s register lessons into a runnable 15-step curriculum — from blank skeleton through screens, scrolling, FCM/NCM, and progressively advanced RRB pixie engines — with a reusable `mega65system.asm`/`mega65macros.asm` library and a PNG→chr/pal asset pipeline, all in Kick Assembler.

[[MEGA65-mega65-tools]] is the official PC-side tool suite: `m65` (hardware swiss-army knife), `mega65_ftp` (SD card transfer), `etherload` (Ethernet deploy, beta), `coretool` (core container files), and `romdiff` (ROM patches) — prebuilt on the Filehost for all three OSes, source actively developed on the `development` branch.

[[MEGA65-mega65-code-snippets]] is the official meta-repo mapping the community GitHub ecosystem: assemblers (BSA, S65 macro toolkit), IDEs (C64Studio, on-device Eleven), third-party toolchains (m65dbg, MEGA65-SDK), example repos, complete games (raycaster, dragonrock, megafighter), and the MiSTer2MEGA65 core-porting framework — the "has someone already built X" index.

[[dansanderson-easyasm65]] covers the on-device path: EasyAsm assembles all 45GS02 instructions with Acme-compatible syntax inside the MEGA65's own screen editor (`MOUNT "EASYASM.D81" : BOOT`, Edit mode, Help-key menu), assembling to memory or disk — its README, embedded in full in the raw capture, is the complete manual.

[[lgblgblgb-xemu]] is the de-facto MEGA65 emulator (SDL2, GPL-2.0, master/next/dev branches) at the center of every workflow here, and [[MEGA65-mega65-core]] is the VHDL source of the machine itself — release 0.97.2 — tracked at brief detail for release/flashing questions, with builder.mega65.org doing automated builds.

[[dansanderson-mega65-symbols]] provides ready-to-include I/O register symbol files for six toolchains (ACME, Kick Assembler, ca65, cc65, Ophis, Rust), generated from `iomap.txt` in the MEGA65 core's VHDL — so register names track real hardware. Including the file for your toolchain at project start eliminates raw `$D0xx` address lookups throughout your source code.

[[MEGA65-mega65-rom-public]] is the public issue tracker and CHANGELOG for the closed-source MEGA65 ROM. The repository has no ROM source code, but its `CHANGELOG.md` is the authoritative version history — the definitive answer to "which ROM version introduced BASIC 65 feature X?" covering releases from 920287 (Jan 2022) through the current stable 920413 (v0.97, April 2025).

[[commodore-65-wikipedia]] provides the historical and hardware-spec baseline for the Commodore 65 prototype (C64DX) that the MEGA65 recreates: CSG 4510 @ 3.54 MHz, VIC-III, dual SID, BASIC 10.0, cancelled 1991. Useful when a MEGA65 register or mode traces back to the original C65 design — for the MEGA65's own implementation see [[MEGA65-mega65-core]] and [[MEGA65-mega65-user-guide]].

[[MEGA65-m65dbg]] is the project's enhanced serial debugger — the step up from the built-in Matrix Mode Monitor when symbolic source-level tracing is needed. It adds symbolic debugging (Ophis/ACME/CC65 symbol maps), hardware breakpoints, step/step-over/finish, memory watch, disassembly with source listings, and PETSCII screenshots over a serial connection to real hardware or a Unix socket to [[lgblgblgb-xemu]]. Borrows FTP and hardware-interaction code directly from [[MEGA65-mega65-tools]].

[[dansanderson-dis65]] adds the reverse-engineering side of the MEGA65 toolchain: a tiny Python CLI that disassembles raw binaries or Commodore PRGs for 6502, 65C02, 65CE02, and 45GS02 targets, auto-detects BASIC 2 vs BASIC 65 load-address conventions, and can emit source shaped for ACME or Kick Assembler. It fits where [[dansanderson.com]] and [[wiebow.mega65.com]] leave off: after you have produced or captured a PRG, dis65 helps turn it back into readable source, while [[MEGA65-m65dbg]] stays focused on live symbolic/debugger sessions.

[[dansanderson-tpn65]] fills a different gap in Dan Sanderson's host-side workflow: instead of debugging or disassembling finished binaries, it lets you write BASIC 65 programs on a PC in an Eleven-like syntax with labels, long variable names, declarations, `#define`/`#ifdef`, optional line numbers, and multi-file source composition, then pipe the generated BASIC text into `petcat -w65` to make a runnable PRG. That makes it a practical bridge between the cross-development guidance in [[dansanderson.com]] and the more on-device editing path of [[dansanderson-easyasm65]].

[[dansanderson-homebrew-mega65]] packages the Mac side of the ecosystem into one Homebrew tap: a cask for M65Connect, a cask for [[lgblgblgb-xemu]]'s `xmega65.app`, and a formula that builds and installs the CLI binaries from [[MEGA65-mega65-tools]]. For macOS users following the setup and deployment flows in [[dansanderson.com]] or [[retrocombs.com]], it turns several manual downloads into ordinary `brew tap` / `brew install` commands and makes PATH setup for the common desktop tools more reproducible.

[[dansanderson-joytest65]] is a focused controller-input lab for the MEGA65: one compact Acme assembly program that turns Dan Sanderson's written controller guidance into directly inspectable code and a visual tester. Its practical value is the register-level recipe it preserves locally — disable IRQs, isolate the keyboard on `$DC00`, read joystick bits from `$DC01` / `$DC00`, drop the MEGA65 to 1 MHz for paddle timing, debounce `$D419` / `$D41A`, and interpret not just one-button sticks but the three-button and five-button conventions as well.

[[dansanderson-mega65-pcfonts]] is a small but practical TCR asset pack for the MEGA65: 43 old-school PC fonts converted into loadable character sets, bundled as a `PCFONTS.D81` browser disk and backed by a Python conversion script. It sits at the hardware-graphics edge of the wiki, showing how Tall ChaRacter mode can be used as a drop-in font system instead of just a curiosity.

[[cc65-cc65]] is the dominant free cross-development suite for 6502-family systems — C compiler, ca65 macro assembler, ld65 linker, archiver, and simulator in one package. For MEGA65 C development it is the foundation layer: [[MEGA65-mega65-libc]] provides the MEGA65-specific runtime on top of cc65, and both [[MEGA65-mega65-fdisk]] and [[MEGA65-mega65-freezemenu]] build with it. It can assemble 45GS02 with `.cpu 45GS02` even when using only the ca65 assembler without the C compiler.

[[dansanderson-bf-mega65]] is Dan Sanderson's Brainf\*ck interpreter for the MEGA65, written in 45GS02 assembly: a compact, self-contained program demonstrating the 45GS02 relocatable base-page register and MEGA65 BASIC/SYS interoperability. It ships as a ready-to-run D81 disk image and serves primarily as a teaching example alongside the richer tutorial content in [[dansanderson.com]] and [[RetroCogs-Mega65Tutorials]].

[[dansanderson-bsa-vscode]] is Dan Sanderson's VS Code extension for the BSA assembler ([[Edilbert-BSA]]): syntax highlighting, error marking, go-to-definition, and symbol listing via an LSP server. It is the IDE companion for developers using BSA for MEGA65 assembly work in VS Code, installable from the `.vsix` release or built from TypeScript source.

[[RetroCogs-GameShell65]] is RetroCogs' KickAssembler game framework for the MEGA65, built around heavy Raster Rewrite Buffer usage. It provides a complete game-loop skeleton: DMA-buffered double-frame rendering, Pixie-based sprites, GameState screen management (title/gameplay/credits), camera, and background map — the most structured open-source game-development starting point for MEGA65 on GitHub. [[RetroCogs-GS65_ShmupExample]] is its companion demo.

[[RetroCogs-GS65_ShmupExample]] is a complete shoot-em-up example game using the [[RetroCogs-GameShell65]] framework, demonstrating player movement, bullets, enemy AI, projectile spawning, and math routines in a working game structure. With 14 commits and 1 fork, it is a practical reference for how GameShell65's RRB/Pixie/DMA pipeline scales to a full game.

[[MirageBD-MegaTool]] is a host-side C utility suite used by RetroCogs in their MEGA65 game workflow: `gfx2code` converts image files to KickAssembler data arrays, `imgconvert` handles image format conversion, and `cruncher` compresses binaries to reduce D81 disk footprint. The repo has no README; purpose is inferred from source filenames.

[[lgblgblgb-megacyc]] is LGB's cycle-timing tester for 45GS02 opcodes, running on both real MEGA65 hardware and [[lgblgblgb-xemu]]. It measures actual clock cycles at 40.5 MHz — data that is not fully documented — and compares Xemu results against hardware, helping validate and improve emulator CPU timing accuracy. Results (CSV/TSV) are committed in the repo's `public/` directory.

[[lgblgblgb-mega80]] (MEGA/80) runs CP/M v2.2 on the MEGA65 by emulating an Intel i8080 CPU in 65xx assembly, with a custom CBIOS dispatching CP/M system calls to native MEGA65 hardware via HALT opcode traps. It is a platform curiosity demonstrating the MEGA65 is fast enough to run a software CPU emulator and a classic operating system; a ready-to-run D81 disk image is in `dist/bin/`.

[[lgblgblgb-mega65-utils]] is LGB's collection of MEGA65-related utilities — both native and cross-platform host tools: `megainfo` for hardware info display, `m65c` for Ethernet-based SD card file management, `eth-tool` for network diagnostics, `navigator` for file browsing, and `c65izer` for C65 compatibility. Status is explicitly WIP; the last commit was in 2022. Complements [[MEGA65-mega65-tools]] for use cases not covered by the official suite.

[[lgblgblgb-c64-sfx-cartridge-player]] is LGB's OPL2/AdLib DRO file player for the C64 (SFX Sound Expander cartridge) and MEGA65 (direct OPL register access). It plays DOSBOX-recorded DRO music files and doubles as a MEGA65 OPL hardware test — a hardware bug in real MEGA65 OPL3 caused no audio output at time of writing (mega65-core issue #232).

[[lgblgblgb-fdc65bas]] is LGB's BASIC65 experiment in reading floppy disk sectors via POKE/PEEK to FDC registers, bypassing the MEGA65 DOS entirely. Two sub-projects: a sector-read hex-dump demo and a simple MS-DOS floppy imager attempt. Primarily a learning exercise documenting the FDC register interface accessible from BASIC65.

[[MEGA65-eleven]] is the on-device BASIC 65 IDE and preprocessor for the MEGA65: boot from `11.D81` to enter the EE full-screen editor, write BASIC without line numbers using labels and long variable names, press F5 to compile and run. It is the natural starting point for MEGA65-native BASIC development; the host-side counterpart is [[dansanderson-tpn65]], and ALT+P posts source to a PC via m65postbox for version control.

[[MEGA65-m65connect]] is the official cross-platform GUI for MEGA65 remote interaction (Windows, Mac, Linux), written in Xojo. It wraps the CLI tools from [[MEGA65-mega65-tools]] in a graphical interface: SD card manager, PRG/ROM/bitstream transfer, ROM configurator, D81 image editor, remote keyboard, and terminal mode. Available as a macOS Homebrew package via [[dansanderson-homebrew-mega65]]; manual at [[mega65.atlassian.net]].

[[MEGA65-mega65-libc]] is the official C library for MEGA65, supporting both [[cc65-cc65]] and Clang/LLVM-MOS toolchains. It provides FAT32 file I/O, POSIX-like directory access, and MEGA65-specific hardware APIs in `include/mega65/`. It is a build dependency of [[MEGA65-mega65-fdisk]] and [[MEGA65-mega65-freezemenu]], and the natural starting point for any new CC65 or LLVM-MOS C project targeting the MEGA65.

[[MEGA65-mega65-weeip]] ports the WeeIP micro TCP/IP stack to the MEGA65's 100Mbit Ethernet controller, enabling TCP, UDP, DHCP, DNS, and multiple sockets. Two example programs are included: a PETSCII BBS terminal and an HTTP file fetcher. Status is work-in-progress, not yet fully production-ready, but it opens the door to networked applications from a physical MEGA65.

[[MEGA65-mega65-examples]] is the official community example repository for the MEGA65: buildable, documented source code examples organized by language (assembly, C, C++, Rust), linked from mega65.org. Each example lives in its own directory with build instructions and an `info.json` descriptor. It is the canonical destination for self-contained, multi-language MEGA65 code samples, complementing the tutorial focus of [[RetroCogs-Mega65Tutorials]] and the meta-index of [[MEGA65-mega65-code-snippets]].

[[MEGA65-mega65-freezemenu]] provides the MEGA65's essential system utilities: FREEZER.M65 (hold RESTORE — freeze/restore machine state, mount disk images), MEGAINFO.M65 (hardware info for bug reports), MONITOR.M65 (inspect frozen memory), AUDIOMIX.M65, SPRITED.M65, ROMLOAD.M65, and MAKEDISK.M65. These files must be installed on the SD card for the MEGA65 to function normally. The project was forked from [[MEGA65-mega65-fdisk]] and shares its FAT32 and HAL layers.

[[MEGA65-mega65-fdisk]] is the SD card FDISK and format utility for MEGA65, partitioning and formatting cards in the layout the hypervisor expects. It requires [[MEGA65-mega65-libc]] as a build dependency and uses [[cc65-cc65]] (bundled as a submodule). [[MEGA65-mega65-freezemenu]] was forked from this project and shares its FAT32, HAL, and screen layers.

[[MEGA65-open-roms]] is the flagship open-source ROM replacement project for C64, C65, and MEGA65 — 292 stars, clean-room reverse-engineered using only secondary sources (books, public documentation), with a `similarity` tool enforcing that no byte sequences above 2 bytes match the original ROMs. It enables free distribution of a fully functional MEGA65 without requiring original Commodore firmware. The MEGA65 core build packages these ROMs; BASIC completeness is the current long-running development focus.

[[MEGA65-c65-specifications]] is the MEGA65 team's corrected and augmented version of the original Commodore C65/C64DX system specification — the primary hardware reference document for the machine the MEGA65 evolved from, covering VIC-III, 4510 CPU, FDC, CIA, BASIC 10, and memory map. It complements the historical background in [[commodore-65-wikipedia]] and the definitive MEGA65 register documentation in [[MEGA65-mega65-user-guide]].

[[MEGA65-mega-basic64]] is an experimental, now-stale (last commit 2019) extension to C64 BASIC 2 adding MEGA65-specific canvas/tile graphics and 256-colour text mode commands (CANVAS, TILE SET, COLOUR, STAMP). It was an early MEGA65 team testbed for exploring VIC-IV graphics from BASIC, showing the tile/canvas design that influenced later MEGA65 feature development. Historical reference rather than active tool.

[[Edilbert-BSA]] is the Bit Shifter Assembler — a single-file C cross-assembler for 6502/65C02/45GS02, released under the Unlicense (public domain). The MEGA65/BSA fork is the version used in official MEGA65 development. BSA occupies the niche of simple, linker-free assembly projects; [[dansanderson-bsa-vscode]] provides VS Code IDE support. [[cc65-cc65]]'s ca65 is the more widely used alternative for projects needing a linker or C compilation.

[[sy2002-MiSTer2MEGA65]] is a GitHub template repository and VHDL framework for porting MiSTer FPGA cores to the MEGA65. MiSTer uses an ARM co-processor running Linux for peripheral I/O (menus, ROM loading, disk mounting); the MEGA65 has none. M2M replaces the ARM/Linux HPS layer with QNICE — a 16-bit SoC that runs on the Artix-7 itself — and provides a complete HDMI pipeline (720p 50/60 Hz), virtual disk drives compatible with MiSTer's sd/img protocol, SD card access, and a configurable on-screen menu. The porting workflow: clone the template, add the MiSTer core as a submodule, implement six VHDL files in `CORE/vhdl/` (main.vhd glue, mega65.vhd wrapper, clk.vhd MMCM clocks, config.vhd menu, globals.vhd constants, keyboard.vhd mapping), and leave `M2M/` untouched. Supports R3 through R6; the reference implementation is the [Commodore 64 for MEGA65](https://github.com/MJoergen/C64MEGA65). The wiki's Ultimate Porting Guide is a 12-phase structured walkthrough with critical traps documented (1.4 MB BRAM budget, PPM-level clock precision, single-line globals.vhd constants for awk scraping). Extends the [[MEGA65-mega65-core]] ecosystem as the entry point for third-party alternate cores.

[[MEGA65-mega65-kbd-pcb]] is the open-hardware KiCad PCB project for the MEGA65 MK-II keyboard — the current R6 production keyboard, designed in 2022 by Paul Gardner-Stephen to replace the MK-I design after COVID chip shortages made the original Lattice FPGA controller unavailable. The MK-II drops the keyboard-side FPGA entirely, replacing it with six I2C IO expanders. Each key connects directly to a GPIO pin, giving full NKRO without per-key diodes; the third keyboard IO line is held low so the [[MEGA65-mega65-core]] can auto-detect keyboard model (MK-I vs MK-II) at boot. The KiCad project files — schematics and 5.5 MB PCB layout — let community members have boards manufactured from the gerber outputs and self-assemble with Cherry MX switches, Würth RGB LEDs, and standard through-hole resistors (CC-BY-SA 4.0).

[[c65gs.blogspot.com]] is Paul Gardner-Stephen's (MEGA65 co-creator) October 2022 post explaining why the MK-II keyboard PCB was redesigned. The Lattice FPGA on the MK-I keyboard became unobtainable in the COVID chip shortage ("Chipaggedon"), so he replaced it with six I2C IO expanders at ~AU$3 each — a design choice that also enabled full NKRO without per-switch diodes, cut per-switch cost by ~AU$0.20, and preserved backward compatibility with MK-I units by holding the third keyboard IO line low (a signal the [[MEGA65-mega65-core]] uses to auto-detect keyboard model). The post is the primary engineering rationale document for the MK-II design; the companion KiCad project files live in [[MEGA65-mega65-kbd-pcb]].

[[9Ib7z64z9N4-mister2mega65-explained]] is Oliver Graf's 18-minute explainer video (March 2022) covering the MiSTer2MEGA65 framework from first principles. The first seven minutes are the clearest accessible spoken introduction to FPGA concepts for the MEGA65 audience: what an FPGA is (not an emulator — it reassembles hardware circuits via bitstream), the MEGA65's Artix-7 hardware (215k LEs, no ARM/HPS), the MiSTer's Cyclone 5 SoC (110k LEs plus a dual-core ARM Cortex-A9 running Linux), and why direct core loading between the two is impossible (the MEGA65 has no Linux/ARM layer to handle SD card, USB, and menus). The video then explains how M2M solves this by providing a VHDL abstraction layer plus QNICE (a 16-bit soft-CPU on the Artix-7) to replace the ARM's role, and demonstrates an early-alpha C64 core running the Comaland demo and Doc Cosmos — the first visible proof-of-concept of the framework. Directly complements the technical depth of [[sy2002-MiSTer2MEGA65]].

[[m-e-g-a.org]] is the Museum of Electronic Games & Art (MEGA eV), a German museum organization whose site serves as an independent MEGA65 community hub. The MEGA65 is a central current focus: the organization hosts beginner workshops, covers batch shipping and firmware news (the December 2022 article announced 920 MEGA65 units with the 2nd batch shipping), and is home to MegaSPUTM — a SCUMM engine re-implementation by community developer kibo (`ki-bo/megasputm`) that runs original LucasArts adventure game assets on MEGA65 hardware. MegaSPUTM exploits MEGA65-specific capabilities — multi-layer RRB graphics (see [[retrocogs.mega65.com]]), digital audio, mouse input, fast loading, and real floppy — in C via the Calypsi compiler; Maniac Mansion is released, a 256-color Monkey Island is in development by M3wP. The site also references [[dansanderson.com]]'s Welcome Guide and the MEGA65 Discord for new owners. Beyond MEGA65, the organization runs retrogaming tournaments and events as part of its general gaming heritage mission.
