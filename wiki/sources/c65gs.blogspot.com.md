The C65GS blog is Paul Gardner-Stephen's personal development journal for the project that became the MEGA65 — "C65GS" (C65 Game System) was the original working name of his FPGA reimplementation of the Commodore 65. Running from January 2014 to the present, it is the single richest primary-source record of how the machine was actually built: CPU and VIC-IV bring-up, BASIC and KERNAL work, hardware revisions, and — across the most recent years — the MEGAphone handheld. This page is an **index** of all 427 posts (title, date, and a one-line gloss each) so the blog is searchable from inside the wiki; follow any permalink to read the full post.

_All claims below are sourced from ../../raw/web/c65gs.blogspot.com.md unless otherwise noted._

## What this is

A reverse-chronological developer blog written by Paul Gardner-Stephen (MEGA65 co-creator and FPGA lead). The early years document the from-scratch bring-up of the C65GS core on FPGA; later posts track MEGA65 hardware and, from roughly 2024 onward, the NLnet-funded MEGAphone (a MEGA65-based handheld with cellular telephony). It is opinionated, detailed, and first-person — closer to an engineering logbook than to polished documentation. Post counts per year match the blog's own archive widget exactly and total 427.

## How to use this index

The post list below is grouped by year (newest first). Each entry is `**[Title](permalink)** (date) — gloss`, where the gloss is the cleaned opening sentence of that post's own feed summary, not an editorial rewrite — so it reflects the author's framing but is sometimes conversational ("The title says it all really."). Use the title plus gloss to decide which post to open; the permalink goes to the full article. For a worked deep-dive example drawn from this blog, see [[c65gs-mk-ii-keyboard]] (the MK-II keyboard PCB design-rationale post, ingested in full and unified with its KiCad repo).

## Themes over time

Derived from the post titles in the index below (treat as a rough map, not an exhaustive taxonomy):

- **2014–2016** — core bring-up: 6502/45GS02 CPU, VIC-IV video, BASIC and KERNAL coming to life, early board prototypes (2014 alone has 85 posts, the most of any year).
- **2017–2020** — maturing hardware and features: memory, DMA, SD card, Ethernet, FPGA core refinement, and steps toward production.
- **2021–2023** — MEGA65 production-era topics including the MK-II keyboard redesign (the "Chipaggedon" chip-shortage rework).
- **2024–2026** — heavy focus on the **MEGAphone** handheld: cellular modem, SMS/voice, power and hardware modules, and software/debugging tooling (LLVM-based).
